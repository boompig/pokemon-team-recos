Usually every team has an HM slave, but this one doesn't. You spread the roles evenly through the team. 'Tis optional which Pokemon you give the HMs to but basically these are the Pokemon that got me through the whole game (Delta Episode as well) in a few days. Mightyena is optional and I traded to get 3 starters in one team (originally 4) so it may not be easy to get but it works. Anyway here it is:

Blaziken @Blazikenite
Ability: Blaze (Speed Boost when mega evolved)
-Brave Bird
-Flare Blitz
-Sky Uppercut
-Blaze Kick/Stone Edge
(Can also temporarily have cut as a Combusken/Torchic, I recommend removing it though)

I choose Torchic as my starter whenever I restart this game because Mega Blaziken is such a beast. Brave bird and Flare Blitz are to deal with Psychic and Flying types (if you aren't running stone edge) and Special Walls. With STAB, Flare blitz becomes 180 power, Sky uppercut and Blaze Kick both become 127.5 power (rounded to 125). Stone edge isn't reliable accuracy-wise but is a very powerful counter for flying.

Gardevoir @Gardevoirite (Mega stone obtainable after delta episode)
Ability: Synchronize (Pixilate when mega evolved)
-Psychic/Psyshock
-Calm Mind
-Magical Leaf (Optional)
-Hyper voice (Turns into fairy type and gets boost with mega gardevoir's ability)

Mega Gardevoir with this set is an all-around beast. All moves are powered up by Calm Mind's Sp.Atk boost. Magical leaf is what I used the whole game through, but wasn't that reliable because of the 60 power. I would like ideas for other possible moves, if anyone has any. Mega Gardevoir also has the Ability Pixilate, which makes all normal type moves Fairy Type AND gives them a 30% boost which brings Hyper Voice to 175.5 power or 220.5 if it's super effective which is insane!

Mightyena @Choice Scarf/Quick Claw
Ability: Intimidate
-Ice Fang
-Crunch
-Take down
-Assurance/Thief/Rock Smash

Mightyena is insanely strong with the STAB Crunch but the other moves are slightly disappointing. I easily wipe Pheobe of the E4's team with the Crunch. Not sure of any replacements, but I am thinking Thief/Snarl would be good to replace assurance because both the items on Mightyena increase speed, making Assurance practically useless.

Torterra @ Soft Sand/Miracle Seed/Yache Berry
Ability: Overgrow
-Wood Hammer
-Crunch
-Razor Leaf/Synthesis
-Earthquake

Nothing much to say about this set except that it is your best bet against Electric types.

Feraligatr @Sea Incense/Wacan Berry
Ability: Torrent
-Crunch
-Strength
-Aqua Tail/Hydro Pump
-Waterfall

Feraligatr's Attack Stat is higher than his Sp.Atk stat so I would recommend running Aqua Tail over Hydro Pump unless you are confident enough to deal with the accuracy and have a +Sp.Atk nature. Strength and Crunch are optional, if you find anything better please tell me but waterfall is a must. I'm sure it is the 2nd strongest physical water move behind aqua tail.

Ludicolo @Amulet Coin/Mystic Water/ Miracle Seed
Ability: Rain Dish
-Fake Out/Giga Drain
-Ice Beam/Blizzard
-Surf/Hydro Pump
-Rain dance

Ludicolo is usually called a useless Pokemon or unworthy or even being a Pokemon, but this set for Ludicolo works well. As flying is a big weakness for the team (Torterra, Ludicolo and Blaziken and even sometimes Gardevoir because of Crobat), I have given Ludicolo ice beam. I do recommend it over blizzard as blizzard has terrible accuracy. My Ludicolo is lvl88 and currently packs fake out, ice beam, surf and rain dance. Easily destroys some of the E4's beasts like Salamence, Altaria, Shiftry and Mandibuzz with a simple Fake Out/Ice beam combo or you can take care of Pokemon like Pheobe's Chandelure and Mega Sableye with a Rain dance/Surf combo.

Thank you for reading this... if you did or No thanks for skipping to the end. Anyway, hope this helps with anyone currently team building.

I went with a team I tried to balance with as many types as I could get, along with some high base stats. Moves are after getting rid of most HM's for E4 and Champ.

Sceptile - Grass (MSceptile - Grass/Dragon)
Ability - Overgrow (MSceptile - Lightning Rod)
Moveset - Leaf Storm, Leaf Blade, X-Scissor, Dragon Claw - can swap Earthquake in for X-Scissor for fire types
Overall, just my favorite of the starters. Type disadvantage is an issue with fire types but can change one move to Earthquake or use Aggron for Surf.

Gardevoir - Psychic/Fairy
Ability - Synchronize
Moveset - Moonblast, Magical Leaf, Draining Kiss, Shadow Ball
Nice dual type Pokemon. Deadly against dragon types and can use Shadow Ball on fellow psychics. Magical Leaf good for rock types and water types.

Aggron - Steel/Rock (MAggron - Steel)
Ability - Rock Head
Moveset - Double Edge, Iron Head, Earthquake, Surf
Rock Head is a must for Double Edge. Really useful to be able to use that powerful attack and have no recoil. Surf is good for dealing with pesky fire types. Same with Earthquake.

Crobat - Poison/Flying
Ability - Inner Focus
Moveset - Acrobatics, Cross Poison, X-Scissor, Dark Pulse
Can deal with psychics with Dark Pulse and X-Scissor. Acrobatics is deadly when not holding an item.

Crawdaunt - Water/Dark
Ability - Shell Armor
Moveset - Sludge Wave, Ice Beam, Dark Pulse, Crabhammer
Fairies are no problem with Sludge Wave. Sludge Wave also good for the grass types, along with Ice Beam. Crabhammer just powerful.

Flygon - Ground/Dragon
Ability - Levitate
Moveset - Boomburst, Flamethrower, Steel Wing, Dragon Claw
Don't evolve Vibrava till level 47 so it can learn Boomburst. Flamethrower is good for ice types and Steel Wing for the fairies.
