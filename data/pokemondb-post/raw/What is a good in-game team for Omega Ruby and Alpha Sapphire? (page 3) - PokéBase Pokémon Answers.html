This is a very balanced one around Mudkip.

Swampert@Swampertite
Ability: Torrent

  • Waterfall
  • Earthquake
  • Hammer Arm
  • Ice Punch

Waterfall and Earthquake are STAB and accurate. Hammer Arm for coverage and Ice Punch takes care of the 4x grass weakness.

Linoone@None
Ability: Pickup

  • Cut
  • Surf
  • Strength
  • Rock Smash

While he is an HM slave, I do recommend you use him in battle as well. When your at the elite four you should forget his HM's though and replace them with more useful coverage moves like Thunderbolt, Ice Beam, Shadow Ball and Return for example. And Pickup is super useful as well, hence why he has no item.

Roserade@Miracle Seed
Ability: Natural Cure

  • Leech Seed
  • Toxic
  • Shadow Ball
  • Giga Drain

Leech Seed and Toxic wears the opponent down. She can restore health with Leech Seed and Giga Drain aswell. Giga Drain is also STAB and Shadow Ball is for type coverage.

Ninetales@Charcoal
Ability: Flash Fire

  • Nasty Plot
  • Will-o-Wisp
  • Energy Ball
  • Flamethrower

Nasty Plot to sharply raise Sp. Attack and Will-o-Wisp to inflict burns and take less physical damage. Energy Ball covers all weaknesses and Flamethrower is solid STAB.

Raichu@Magnet
Ability: Static

  • Nuzzle
  • Quick Attack
  • Thunderbolt
  • Grass Knot

Nuzzle is simply a better version of Thunder Wave, dealing damage as well. Quick attack for priority and Thunderbolt is STAB. Grass Knot covers Ground types.

Skarmory@Leftovers
Ability: Sturdy

  • Fly
  • Iron Head
  • Toxic
  • Protect

Fly and Iron Head are STAB. Toxic and Protect wear the opponent down effectively, a very defensive Pokemon.

Items are only examples and Abilities are only recommended.

Electrode (Soundproof): Hyper Beam/Rollout, Electro Ball, Thunderbolt, Magnet Rise
Mega Altaria (Pixilate): Fly, Draco Meteor, Take Down, Dragon Claw

Crawdaunt (Hyper Cutter): Surf, Ice Beam, Crunch, Crabhammer
Sceptile (Overgrow): Dual Chop, Energy Ball, Leaf Blade, Swords Dance

Torchic chosen
Magnezone (Magnet Pull): Thunderbolt, Tri Attack, Flash Cannon, Thunder Wave
Beautifly (Swarm): Quiver Dance, Energy Ball, Psychic, Bug Buzz
Flygon: Fly, Earthquake, Rock Slide, Dragon Claw
Mega Sharpedo: Surf, Ice Fang, Crunch, Waterfall
Gardevoir (Synchronize): Moonblast, Shadow Ball, Energy Ball, Psychic
Blaziken (Blaze): Flamethrower/Overheat, Sky Uppercut, Bulk Up, Blaze Kick

Mudkip chosen
Cosplay Pikachu (Static): Icicle Crash, Thunderbolt, Iron Tail, Brick Break
Roselia/Roserade (Poison Point): Energy Ball, Sludge Bomb, Dazzling Gleam, Safeguard
Salamence (Intimidate): Flamethrower, Fly, Dragon Claw, Crunch
Heracross (Guts): Megahorn, Earthquake, Close Combat, Shadow Claw
Aggron (Sturdy): Hone Claws, Earthquake, Rock Slide, Iron Head
Mega Swampert (Torrent): Surf, Ice Beam, Earthquake, Stone Edge

Now, here's the reasoning behind all my suggestions!
With Treecko, you need anti-poison, anti-bug, anti-fire, and anti-ice strategies. Since you have an actually good grass type, selecting your electric type really isn't that important, so you could just teach Thunderbolt to a non-Electric type. Mega Altaria will serve as your flying type. I equipped her with mostly physical attacks because they are not affected by Draco Meteor. Since Pixilate converts moves to Fairy, Altaria's Take Down gets STAB. Camerupt is our fire type, strong against many of Metagross's attacks. So, he'll be perfect to use in the final battle against Steven - and in a team replete with medium slow and slow levelling rates, having a medium fast Pokemon is great. Sandslash is our ground type, and supposed to balance out against most fire and poison types. Crawdaunt is our water type. Give him Surf for HM and Ice Beam for Dragon types. He learns Crunch - teach it to him at all costs due to both STAB and coverage against Phoebe's ghost Pokemon. And lastly, we have Sceptile. The good thing is that he has wonderful Sp Attack and decent attack. The problem is that most of his coverage moves are physical, so you just have to make the most of it with Dual Chop, Leaf Blade, and Swords Dance.

With Torchic, you need water, grass, and dark to deal with rock, ground, fairy, water, and psychic. It's also incredibly important who you use as your electric type. This makes Magnezone a perfect fit. Flygon triples as a rock type (okay, he's really a ground type), a Flyer, and a dragon type. Give him Fly, Dragon Claw, Earthquake, and Rock Slide (no need for Crunch, and you'll see why). Sharpedo has extremely good attack (despite his pathetic poor defenses), and I would recommend teaching it Surf since it isn't too bad of a move for his special attack. Beautifly is here to serve as a high-powered special attacker, and so is Gardevoir. Both Beautifly and Gardevoir will learn Energy Ball as both these guys have better coverage moves than grass types. And even though Beautifly is really weak to Rock types, it has the speed and special attack to OHKO most of em in one hit. And I'm teaching Blaziken one special move because Metagross won't go down in one hit even from a supereffective physical move. I also threw Beautifly in here to speed up the leveling process.

With Mudkip, your primary concern is grass. So, a poison type could work well - enter Roselia and Sludge Bomb. She doubles as a grass type. Salamence is probably your best bet here as a fire type (Even though it's dragon and flying) since he's so versatile. A Flamethrower from Salamence will do lots of damage to Steven's team. The problem with him is that he appears so late in the game, but he's worthwhile all the same. Heracross is quite useful as a fighting type due to power and speed. Aggron is here to neutralize weaknesses to grass, and give him Sturdy so he can survive Focus Blast or Earth Power. Cosplay Pikachu is here because you get him quite early in the game, and although he can't evolve or anything, he gets a special move (which gets powered up by a free Light Ball on route 120). But this move HAS to be Icicle Crash to deal with ground - otherwise you may as well teach him a regular move. And lastly, Swampert. Mega Evolve him so that his Surf and Ice Beam attacks actually do damage. I normally would be suggesting Ice Punch and Aqua Tail instead of Surf and Ice Beam, but you can't get Ice Punch before the Pokemon League, so Ice Beam will have to do for now. Happy playing!!!

Sceptile @ Sceptilite/Miracle Seed
Trait - Overgrow > Lightningrod

  • Leaf Storm
  • Leaf Blade
  • Dragon Claw
  • Rock Slide

Leaf Storm is a very strong move and the Sp. A drop isn't a problem as this set is mainly physical, Leaf Blade and Dragon Claw (when mega) are both STAB and Rock Slide cover fire, ice, flying and bug and has a decent flinch chance.

Gallade @ Galladite/Expert Belt
Trait - Steadfast > Inner Focus

  • Brick Break/Close Combat
  • Night Slash
  • Ice Punch/Thunder Punch
  • Psycho Cut

Both Close Combat and Brick Break are useful. Close Combat is stronger with less PP and an additional effect of lowering both defences while Brick Break breaks screens but is less powerful but will still do good damage with high base Attack. Night Slash is ghost type coverage and Ice Punch/Thunder Punch are flying type coverage.

Tentacruel @ Black Sludge
Trait - Clear Body

  • Surf
  • Sludge Wave
  • Waterfall
  • Ice Beam

Surf is needed for the story and is STAB, Sludge wave has a poison chance and is STAB, Waterfall is also needed for the Story and is STAB with a flinch chance and Ice Beam has a freeze chance and is ground coverage. (Dive is also an alternative for Waterfall and is needed for the story but I recommend Waterfall)

Altaria @ Altarianite/Leftovers
Trait - Natural Cure > Pixilate

  • Fly
  • Flamethrower
  • Dragon Pulse
  • Return/Moonblast

Fly is useful for getting around and is STAB, Flamethrower has a burn chance and covers ice, Dragon Pulse is STAB and use return if you are going to Mega Evolve as it gets STAB from pixilate but if you don't Mega Evolve use Moonblast.

Camerupt @ Cameruptite/Charcoal
Trait - Solid Rock > Sheer Force

  • Flamethrower
  • Earthquake
  • Rock Slide
  • Eruption

Flamethrower has a burn chance and is STAB, Earthquake is STAB, Rock Slide has a flinch chance (Rock Slide could be swapped for a status move moves like Curse, Amnesia or Yawn for example) and Eruption is good for doing big damage when Camerupt hasn't taken any damage himself.

Magnezone @ Magnet/Metal Coat
Trait - Sturdy

  • Charge Beam/Metal Sound
  • Thunderbolt
  • Thunder Wave/Tri-Attack
  • Flash Cannon

Charge Beam has a huge chance of raising it's already very high Sp. A but Metal Sound harshly lowers the opponents Sp. D with less accuracy though. Thunder Wave makes up for the speed that he lacks but Tri-Attack has a chance to either burn, freeze or paralyse the opponent. Flash Cannon has a chance of decreasing the opponents Sp.D and has STAB and Thunderbolt is STAB.

Team Preview: Swampert, Gardevoir, Hariyama, Crobat, Manetric, Absol


Swampert (Mudkipz) @ Swampertite/Macho Brace
Ability: Torrent > Swift Swim

  • Surf/Hammer Arm/Rock Slide/Rain Dance
  • Waterfall
  • Earthquake
  • Ice Beam

Swampert is a beast both Offensively and Defensively because of it's excellent typing and good stats, and it helps in many parts of the game like with Wattson and Flannery for example. Surf is important for the story but his other moves provide coverage and are also physical and Rain Dance activates Swift Swim in his mega form. Waterfall gains STAB as does Earthquake and Ice Beam takes care of Swampert's only weakness; Grass.


Gardevoir (Athena) @ Gardevoirite/Life Orb
Ability: Synchronise > Pixilate

  • Psychic
  • Shadow Ball
  • Moonblast
  • Calm Mind/Focus Blast/Thunderbolt

Gardevoir is a very good Pokémon with a large movepool and very high Special stats. Gardevoir also does very well against the evil teams as it can easily beat Poison and Dark types. Psychic and Moonblast do heaps with STAB and Gardevoir's high Sp. A and Shadow Ball is for coverage. Calm Mind increases Sp. A and Sp. D making Gardevoir a monster and Focus Blast and Thunderbolt are for coverage.


Hariyama (Yokozuna) @ Leftovers
Ability: Thick Fat

  • Close Combat
  • Bulk Up
  • Stone Edge
  • Earthquake

Hariyama, just like Swampert, is very good on offence and defence and helps out on important parts of the game like Norman, Glacia and of course Steven. Close Combat will deal huge damage but drops it's defeces by one stage but Hariyama's huge HP blanks that and will do even more after Bulk Up raising his Attack and Defence. Stone Edge and Earthquake are for coverage and are overall great moves.


Crobat (Acrobat) @ Black Sludge
Ability: Inner Focus

  • Cross Poison
  • Fly
  • Steel Wing
  • U-Turn/X-Scissor/Roost

Crobat is a very fast and well-rounded Pokémon who is definately worth the struggle of Zubat, while Crobat isn't super-useful against the Gyms, E4 and Champion, it is still a good Pokémon non the less. Cross Poison has a high Critical Hit ratio and a poison chance which is handy and also gains STAB as does Fly which also gets you around the reigon. Steel Wing is for coverage as are X-Scissor and U-Turn and Roost heals half of Crobat's max HP but it loses it's Flying type until the end of the turn. Also it's nickname, Acrobat, A-Crobat. LOLOLOLOL.


Manectric (Raiju) @ Manecricite/Metronome
Ability: Lightning Rod > Intimidate

  • Volt Switch
  • Thunderbolt
  • Flamethrower
  • Double Team

Manectric is a very fast and very strong but unfortunately lacks bulk however getting Intimidate as he Mega Evolves does make up for this and he is usefull for a few battles, mainly Winona and Wallace. Volt Switch allows a free hit before you swap instead of just swapping out and Thunderbolt will be your main STAB with a paralyse chance. Flamethrower is for coverage and is a good move and Double Team will increase evasiveness which can allow free turns and with Manectric's high speed it'll likely be able to get at least one off.


Absol (Eclipse) @ Absolite/Muscle Band
Ability: Super Luck > Magic Bounce

  • Swords Dance
  • Night Slash
  • Psycho Cut
  • Rock Slide/Aerial Ace/X-Scissor/Shadow Claw

Absol is definitely a glass cannon having very small bulk but very high attack but if used in the right circumstances and it will be a force to be reckoned with. Swords Dance pretty much means you are ready to sweep but having low defences makes this a risky but satisfying play. Night Slash is main STAB and has a high Critical Hit ratio combined with Super luck this move will be a critical almost all the time, same goes for Psycho Cut which is for coverage. All the other moves are for coverage.

This is the team I used in Omega Ruby.
I have listed HMs on all the Pokemon along with another move because I don't like using HM slaves. once I beat the main game I replace the HMs
BLAZIKEN

Ability: Blaze (Speed boost is better but it is difficult to obtain)
Item: Blazikenite or life orb
Moves:
- flare blitz
- rock smash (HM)/high jump kick
- stone edge/hidden power grass
- swords dance/protect (for speed boost)
Obviously you will want speed boost but it doesn't really matter if you intend to mega evolve it because it gets it then anyway. Flare blitz is the reliable STAB move and high jump kick is just another good stab option, but slightly risky, especially with the life orb. stone edge is good for dealing with flying types and some all round reliable coverage. hidden power grass is weaker than stone edge, but it covers two of blaziken's weakness. swords dance is a good setup move, but protect can help you raise your speed much easier.
BRELOOM

Ability: Effect spore (Again, Technician is better but more difficult to get)
Item: Life orb
Moves:
- seed bomb
- drain punch
- strenght (HM)/ mach punch
- swords dance/spore/toxic
Seed bomb is the reliable STAB damage, mach punch can deal with fast attackers with a lower defense very easily and drain punch recovers health. Swords dance is good for setting up breloom but in the long term spore or toxic may be more useful. Keep this thing away from flying types as it cant hit them or take hits from them.
SKARMORY

Ability: Sturdy
Item: Leftovers
Moves:
- fly (HM)/iron head
- roost
- whirlwind
- spikes
Skarmory has everything. Great defense, capable of doing damage, recovery and entry hazards. Fly is good enough as an attack but iron head can do better. Roost, leftovers and sturdy are great for stalling. whirlwind allows Skarmory to switch out anything resisting it and spikes is self explanatory.
ABSOL

Ability: Justified
Item: Absolite or life orb
Moves:
- cut (HM)/knock off
- sucker punch
- swords dance
- superpower
Once you get a swords dance off that sucker is deadly. In the main game knock off is useless, but if you want to play competitively, this can be useful. Superpower is for coverage.
FROSLASS

Ability: Snow cloak
Item: wise glasses
Moves:
- shadow ball
- ice beam
- destiny bond
- pain split
Froslass is a strong Pokemon, but is mainly here to cover the flying type weaknesses this team has and beat Drake. Ice beam and shadow ball are good STAB moves, while destiny bond and pain split can both be incredible options to have.
MILOTIC

Ability: competitive
Item: Leftovers
Moves:
- surf (HM)/Hydro pump
- dive (HM)/scald
- ice beam
- waterfall (HM)/recover
hydro pump does good damage and scald has the chance to burn. ice beam can kill grass types. it might look a bit like a HM slave, but dive and waterfall are both obtained very late in the game and surf can still do good damage.

OTHER OPTIONS

Froslass: Magnezone/Manectric
Absol: Dusknoir
Skarmory: Crobat/Swellow/Salamance/Altaria
Breloom: Roserade/Sceptile

Team Preview: Blaziken, Gardevoir, Swellow, Roserade, Flygon, Sharpedo/Milotic


Blaziken @ Blazikenite
Trait: Blaze > Speed Boost

  • Flare Blitz
  • High Jump Kick
  • Brave Bird
  • Earthquake/Stone Edge

Flare Blitz deals massive damage but it will do quite a lot of recoil damage, High Jump Kick is very risky but when it hits it does a lot of damage. Brave Bird also does a lot of recoil damage but it is very good for coverage and finally Earthquake and Stone Edge are excellent for coverage.

You can get the TM for Earthquake at the Seafloor Cavern and the TM for Stone Edge at the Lilycove department store.


Gardevoir @ Gardevoirite
Trait: Synchronize > Pixialte

  • Psychic
  • Moonblast
  • Shadow Ball

Alright this is gonna be a long one but I believe it's a really great team. So I've played both Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire a couple of times and I've found a team that works extremely well overall both in game and competitively. Sorry, no pictures.

-Flare Blitz -> Best physical fire type move in the game unless you're bothered by the slight drawback of recoil. 

                    (Blaze kick is then an alternative)

-Sky Uppercut-> Good Fighting type move, gains Stab as well; Better than dynamicpunch in my opinion because the extra power isn't worth the fact that it never hits. 

                    (Brick Break is the alternative or even double kick if you wanna bypass sturdy ability)

-ThunderPunch-> This is your coverage move if for some reason you find yourself against a water type and don't have a grass or electric Pokemon
-Protect-> Useful move to scout what your opponents moves are and serves as a free turn to get one free speed boost off on its Mega
ITEM: Blazikenite

Next comes GYARADOS. Fantastic water type and it's fairly easy to acquire. Get an old rod in Dewford and go fish off the coast. Has a pretty standard Gyarados moveset but i'll explain just incase.
-Waterfall-> Possibly the best physical water type attack in my opinion plus it gains stab and serves as an HM.
(Aqua Tail could be an alternative if that's a preference)
-Ice Fang-> This is more of a filler move and works great against Drakes' dragon types except for kingdra. Learns it early on and fairly powerful and can cause flinching, or freeze them.
-Dragon Dance-> This is what makes Gyarados extremely dangerous. Drop one or two of these and he becomes a well rounded sweeper, being able to take out most types.
-Earthquake-> This is your coverage move against his main weakness, electric, but is also just a good move to have on your Pokemon.
DISCLAIMER: Only use gyarados against electric types or anything you suspect to have an electric move on if you have at least one dragon dance because a lot of electric type Pokemon are fast.
ITEM: Focus Sash

AGGRON comes in as the Physically defensive tank on this team. Only thing to really keep it away from are fighting and ground types as they are 4x effective. However, Aggron and his evolution line pretty can pretty much tank and work through most of the gyms such as Mauville's, Petalburg's, and Fortree's.
-Stone Edge-> Good stab attack that packs a lot of power. Only issue is that it may not hit when you really need it to due to its lower accuracy. If reliability is what you're looking for then just change it to rock slide.
-Heavy Slam-> Aggron is one of the heaviest Pokemon in the game. Plus heavy slam is steel so it gains stab.
-Earthquake-> once again a pretty good move to have and it just seems right.
-Iron Defense-> Honestly, Aggron already has a high defense stat, but if you throw up 2 or 3 of these, you will be able to shrug off even a close combat or an earthquake.
ITEM: Aggronite

Never mind Gallade, because there's already a fighting type, throw GARDEVOIR on there. Although I would highly recommend a Gallade on any team that needs a fighting type, Gardevoir has it's advantages. It serves as your strong fairy type and a good psychic type that makes this team fairly well rounded.
-Moonblast-> Stab and there's a chance it reduces Sp. Atk. by one stage. Overall just a great move
-Psychic-> Also stab and it takes care of those pesky poison types while also having a chance to reduce the targets' Sp. Def. stat by one stage.
-Shadow Ball-> Filler move that helps against other psychic types
-Calm Mind-> This turns Gardevoir into an all out boss. Even just one of these dials up Gardevoir's power and makes it an extremely lethal special attacker.
ITEM: Gardevoirite

RAICHU is the electric type on this team and adds a touch of speed other than Blaziken. Although Raichu is only available after you deal with Kyogre/Groudon, it levels up pretty quickly and if you use the PokeFinder to find one with thunderpunch, you're all set.
-ThunderPunch-> Obviously a great Physical electric move, better than spark in my opinion.
(This can be swapped for Wild Charge if you want your Raichu to pack a little extra punch at the price of recoil)
-Thunder Wave-> This is mainly for capturing Pokemon and slowing down any opponents that may still be faster than you.
-Brick Break-> Filler move and is just a good way to deal with Normal, Rock, or Dark Types.
-Agility-> Allows Raichu to become even faster.
ITEM: Light Ball

Acrobatics hits like a truck without an item, and STAB makes it hit even harder. It is learned at level 35 by Crobat. Brave Bird is an option, but requires you to DexNav Zubat, as it is an Egg Move. It comes down to preference. Cross Poison is a Poison STAB move and is extremely strong. You must bring Crobat to the move relearner for it to learn Cross Poison. X-Scissor is there for Coverage. It is a TM obtained in Victory Road. This video should help. Fly is there for the utility and possible second Flying STAB.

Ninetales @Charcoal (Location)
Obtained as a Vulpix at Mt. Pyre.
• Flamethrower
• Nasty Plot
• Extrasensory
• Dark Pulse

Flamethrower is learned at level 36 by Vulpix. A must have STAB for any special fire type, it does its job very well. it is recommended to evolve Vulpix as soon as it learns this, as you don't miss out on too many good moves. The Fire Stone is located in the Fiery Path, but requires the use of Strength. This video should help. Nasty Plot requires you to bring Ninetales to the move relearner in order for it to learn it. This move provides you with a powerful sweeping capability. If you DexNav for a Hidden Ability Vulpix (Drought) then you have an offensive monster on your team. Extrasensory is learned at level 31 by Vulpix. This is basic coverage and hits hard. Dark Pulse is a TM. Even more coverage.
